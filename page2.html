Interview Questions

1. Have you ever noticed frogs on the sidewalk or road near the pond during or after rain? 2. How did you feel when you encountered frogs outside the pond area? 3. Do you think it's a problem worth addressing? Why or why not? 4. Have you or someone you know ever accidentally stepped on or run over a frog? 5. Do you feel like there are enough protections in place for the frogs or local wildlife?

Fishbone Diagram

Point of View (POV) statement

A local Sheridan College, that lives in the same neighborhood needs a way to prevent frogs from ending up on sidewalks and roads at night and after it rains as the fence that encloses their habitat is ineffective with no visible signs or infrastructure protecting the frogs or raising public awareness.

Secondary Research

Source 1

Amphibian road mortality drops by over 80% with wildlife underpasses, study shows
By: University of Vermont
Read Article

What I Learned:

  • Road deaths can be reduced by over 80% using wildlife underpasses or fences.
  • These help frogs cross roads safely, especially at night or in rain.

Connection to My Problem:

Frogs from the nearby pond are being stepped on or run over. The fence isn't enough. Adding guiding systems like in the study could help protect them.

Source 2

Effects of road mortality and mitigation measures on amphibian populations
By: Trevor J C Beebee
PubMed Link
Wiley Full Text

What I Learned:

  • Roads are a major cause of frog deaths worldwide.
  • Near migration routes, roads cause population declines and isolation.
  • Solutions like tunnels and awareness campaigns help.

Connection to My Problem:

This confirms my local frog issue isn't unique. If people don't care or know, the deaths will continue. The research supports the need for solutions now.
